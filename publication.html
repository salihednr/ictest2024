Plagiarism - Guidlines

IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) follows strict guidelines to prevent plagiarism in its international conferences Click Here More Details.

1. Originality Check: Authors are required to submit original work that has not been published elsewhere. IEEE conferences often employ plagiarism detection software to identify any instances of content duplication. 2. Citation and Attribution: Proper citation and attribution are essential. Authors must give credit to all original sources and ideas that have influenced their work. Direct quotes and paraphrased content should be appropriately referenced.

3. Self-Plagiarism: Authors should avoid self-plagiarism, which involves reusing substantial parts of their own previously published work without proper citation or indication of prior publication. Proper References: All references and sources cited in the paper should be valid and accurately listed in the bibliography. Incomplete or incorrect citations can be considered a form of plagiarism.

4. Acknowledgment of Collaboration: If the research is conducted in collaboration with others or supported by external organizations, the contribution and support should be clearly acknowledged.

5. Ethical Use of Others' Work: Authors should not use copyrighted materials (e.g., images, figures, or tables) without obtaining appropriate permissions or licenses. Proper permissions and attributions are required for any such material used in the paper.

6. Review Process: Submitted papers undergo a thorough peer-review process. Reviewers are instructed to check for any signs of plagiarism and report any concerns to the conference organizers.

7. Publication Ethics: Authors should comply with the publication ethics guidelines set forth by IEEE. This includes ensuring that all co-authors have contributed significantly to the research and are aware of the paper submission.
