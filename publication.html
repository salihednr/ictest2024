Plagiarism - Guidlines

IEEE (Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers) follows strict guidelines to prevent plagiarism in its international conferences Click Here More Details.

1. Originality Check: Authors are required to submit original work that has not been published elsewhere. IEEE conferences often employ plagiarism detection software to identify any instances of content duplication. 2. Citation and Attribution: Proper citation and attribution are essential. Authors must give credit to all original sources and ideas that have influenced their work. Direct quotes and paraphrased content should be appropriately referenced.

3. Self-Plagiarism: Authors should avoid self-plagiarism, which involves reusing substantial parts of their own previously published work without proper citation or indication of prior publication. Proper References: All references and sources cited in the paper should be valid and accurately listed in the bibliography. Incomplete or incorrect citations can be considered a form of plagiarism.

4. Acknowledgment of Collaboration: If the research is conducted in collaboration with others or supported by external organizations, the contribution and support should be clearly acknowledged.

5. Ethical Use of Others' Work: Authors should not use copyrighted materials (e.g., images, figures, or tables) without obtaining appropriate permissions or licenses. Proper permissions and attributions are required for any such material used in the paper.

6. Review Process: Submitted papers undergo a thorough peer-review process. Reviewers are instructed to check for any signs of plagiarism and report any concerns to the conference organizers.

7. Publication Ethics: Authors should comply with the publication ethics guidelines set forth by IEEE. This includes ensuring that all co-authors have contributed significantly to the research and are aware of the paper submission.

ICTEST-2024

The ICTEST-2024 conference covers all aspects of intelligent and smart computing systems design, including software and hardware. IEEE Kerala Section is a technical co-sponsor of the conference. Attendees can learn about current research and development in Information Technology at ICTEST-2024. In addition to Intelligent Systems and Applications, Big Data Processing, Internet of Things and Blockchain, Secure Information Processing, Cloud Computing, Edge and Fog Computing, Robotics and automation, Scalable Computing, Neuromorphic Engineering, Multimedia Systems, Electronic Design Automation, Emerging Technologies, Healthcare systems, Power and Energy Systems, and more, ICTEST'24 covers a wide range of topics. Accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion into IEEE Xplore subject to meeting IEEE Xplore’s scope and quality requirements.

Paper Submission

Authors are invited to submit full-length original, unpublished papers in IEEE proceedings format, along with an abstract of at most 250 words and a maximum of five keywords. The papers are limited to six (6) pages, without incurring extra page charges. The paper may be extended to eight (8) pages. In such a case, extra page charges Rs. 500 per page will be applied. To enable blind review, the author list should be omitted from the main document. Previously published papers or papers currently under review for other conferences/journals should NOT be submitted and will not be considered. We will follow the plagiarism policy of IEEE.

Paper Publication

Every accepted paper MUST have at least one author registered to the conference by the time the camera-ready paper is submitted; and at least one of the authors is also expected to attend the conference and present the paper. Papers not presented at the conference will not be included in the final program or in the digital proceedings. Accepted papers will be submitted for inclusion into IEEE Xplore subject to meeting IEEE Xplore’s scope and quality requirements. The manuscript as a single PDF is to be submitted online through Easychair. Authors of the accepted papers will be informed by email.
